Signing MOU with Egyptian Fajr and Jordanian Fajr to establish CNG fueling stations and transport CNG to industrial and commercial activities in Jordan

Within the framework of Ministry of Petroleum’s policy to enhance the Egyptian presence in petroleum and gas projects in the State of Jordan, GASTEC signed during June 2018, a memorandum of understanding with the Egyptian Fajr and Jordanian Fajr in order to establish CNG fueling stations and transport CNG to feed industrial and commercial activities in the Kingdom of Jordan.

The MOU involved preparing technical and economic studies required to implement projects and applications of using CNG in the cities of Aqaba - Amman - Zarqa - Irbid, and forming working teams undertaking permanent and continuous coordination of such projects and applications. As well as availing of the technical capabilities and cadres of the three companies as to implement, operate and maintain CNG projects, including the establishment of fueling stations and CNG conversion centers. In addition to the transportation of CNG by special tankers to feed off- grid areas of industrial or commercial or domestic activity, confirming the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan’s tendency to encourage adopting alternative fuels aiming at rationalizing energy consumption and preserving the environment.

Signing MOU with Bro Gas Company to implement CNG bulk transportation projects by Mother-Daughter Station Technology

In pursuance of implementing the Ministry of Petroleum’s mandate for GASTEC to start the implementation of CNG transport projects to feed industrial and commercial activities in Jordan, during October 2018, GASTEC signed a memorandum of understanding with Pro Gas and GMC for specialized services on the Jordanian side to execute CNG transport project for industrial and commercial facilities in the State of Jordan through the use of Mother-daughter station technology.

In this context, GASTEC received offers from a number of commercial and industrial facilities in the State of Jordan to supply it with CNG. A committee was dispatched to make field visits to such sites to find out the required works and CNG quantities needed to feed such facilities and determine the costs of implementation and making technical and economic studies for the project are underway to start-up the implementation procedures.

Implementing the first CNG bulk transportation project to a residential neighborhood in cooperation with Town Gas Company

In pursuance of implementing CNG transporting projects to facilities and areas that cannot be supplied with gas by traditional methods due its remoteness or lack of economic viability. The Company completed the first project to transport CNG to a residential neighborhood in New Cairo in cooperation with Town Gas, until the completion of the gas pipeline project.

Operating Steel Factories Furnaces with CNG

The Company finished preparing and feeding iron and steel factories affiliated to Egyptian Steel Company with CNG by installing 2 CNG units with a total storage capacity of 600 m3 (300 m3 per unit). In addition, a pressure reducing unit from 220 bar to 70 mbar was installed in order to operate the oven's lighters. As well as installing an automatic cooling line for the storage group aiming at cooling the tanks in case of increasing the temperature, as to ensure the safety of tanks under any operation conditions.
