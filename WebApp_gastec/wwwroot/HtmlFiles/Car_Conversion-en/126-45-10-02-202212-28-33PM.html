Conversion Systems Types:

Firstly: The Carburetor System:

It is a conversion system installed in vehicles run by a carburetor system. The shift between gasoline and CNG is manually via a conversion switch. CNG is incorporated through a mixer installed at the top of the carburetor, and has a gasoline solenoid in order to close the gasoline pathway in case of using CNG.

 

Secondly: The open circuit system:

 

It is a conversion system for vehicles running by an electronic injection system, in which there is no tailpipe sensor. The shift is carried out automatically when starting the engine and reaching to 1500 check per minute. Moreover, a separation device closes the gasoline sprinklers in case of using CNG.

Thirdly: The developed circuit system:

It is a conversion system for vehicles running by an electronic injection system, in which there is a tailpipe sensor. The shift is carried out automatically when starting the engine and reaching to 1500 check per minute. Moreover, a separation device closes the gasoline sprinklers in case of using CNG and a deceptive device is installed to point at the tailpipe sensor while using CNG.

Fourthly: Direct Injection conversion system:

 

It is a conversion system for electronic injection vehicles, consists of special gas control unit, CNG is incorporated by gas spray ruler. CNG quantities are controlled electronically through a special program on a laptop characterized by saving fuel consumption and performance equals to gasoline. Gasoline converted automatically to CNG after reaching the appropriate temperature by the engine also reaching number of windings adjusted by the special program. It is considered one of the latest vehicles' conversion systems.
